✈️ Airplane — 3D Mode Controls

These settings only affect the airplane in 3D Cesium mode. They have no effect on the car or bus.

🐢 Sluggish / Stable ⚡ Twitchy / Responsive

Scales how fast the plane rolls and pitches in response to joystick or keyboard input. Lower = more stable and forgiving; higher = sharper, more aggressive control response. Default: 1.00x.

🐌 Slow build-up 🚀 Instant power

Multiplies the engine's maximum thrust. Higher values mean the plane reaches top speed much faster; lower values give a slow, realistic spool-up. Does not affect drag or stall speed. Default: 1.00x.

🚀 High-Speed Rendering Optimizations

Eliminates frame drops and GPU overload when flying at high speed. Mirrors how Google Earth handles fast camera movement — dynamically trades tile detail for smooth framerate.

Master toggle. When ON, all five systems below activate automatically based on your aircraft speed and altitude.

Active subsystems when enabled:

📐 Dynamic SSE: Raises screen-space error as speed increases (1× at idle → 4× at 300 kts), forcing Cesium to load lower-detail tiles. Smoothly restores quality when you slow down.

🌫️ Far-Clip + Fog: Pulls in the far-clip plane based on altitude and speed, hiding distant tiles. Density-scaled fog naturally conceals the shortened render distance.

💾 Dynamic Memory: Scales the tile cache upward at cruise speed (prefetch buffer) and downward at idle (reduce GPU VRAM pressure). Keeps preloadFlightDestinations active at all times.

🧵 Physics Isolation: Ground-height raycasts are throttled to ~12 fps (cached between calls). HUD DOM writes drop to 20 fps. No heavy synchronous work runs on the main thread every frame.

🛡️ FPS Guard: Monitors live framerate. If it drops below 45 fps, Cesium's dynamicScreenSpaceErrorDensity is boosted aggressively until the framerate recovers.

🎮 GP3DT Quality & Rendering

Only applies when Google Photorealistic 3D Tiles is active. Changes take effect immediately.

🧱 Strict (clip-proof) 🌉 Lenient (fly under bridges)

How far below a GP3DT surface (building roof, bridge deck, or terrain) the plane can travel before being snapped back up. Default 1.5 m stops clipping through real ground. Raise to ~30–60 m to fly under bridges and overpasses without being ejected upward. Setting it very high (150 m+) effectively disables the floor — the plane can pass through terrain entirely.
